About

What Is petexpenses.com?

Your pet-finance research tool · Updated June 2026

petexpenses.com is a pet cost research and budgeting tool for US pet owners. We help you understand what your dog or cat actually costs — not some national average, but a figure adjusted for your pet’s breed, weight, age, activity level, and diet type.

Most pet-cost calculators give you one number. We give you a line-by-line breakdown — food, vet care, insurance, grooming, supplies, and extras — sourced from APPA, AVMA, NAPHIA, BLS, and industry surveys.

Our focus

Pet finances & budget transparency. We are not a generic pet blog. Our mission is to bring the same analytical rigor to pet ownership that you would apply to a mortgage or car payment.

What We Do

  • Breed-adjusted cost estimates — A Chihuahua and a Great Dane do not cost the same. Our calculator adjusts for size, breed-specific health risks, and lifespan.
  • Side-by-side comparisons — Thinking about a second dog or switching from kibble to raw? Our compare tool lets you stack budgets.
  • Data-backed articles — Every number on this site links back to a published source. We cite APPA, AVMA, NAPHIA, BLS, and peer-reviewed veterinary journals.
  • Free, no signup — No account creation, no paywall, no newsletter spam. Your data stays on your device.

Who This Is For

  • Prospective owners who want to know if they can afford a specific breed
  • Current owners who want to optimize their pet budget
  • Veterinary and insurance researchers looking for aggregated cost data
  • Pet industry analysts tracking spending trends

Our Values

  • Transparency — Every estimate includes a range and a confidence note. We flag data gaps honestly.
  • No pet insurance bias — We do not steer users toward any particular insurer or product. Our calculator shows costs with and without insurance so you can decide.
  • Privacy-first — We do not sell your data. See our privacy policy.

Why We Built This

The pet industry in the United States exceeds $150 billion annually, yet most owners go in blind on costs. Veterinary inflation is running at 5.3% year-over-year (BLS, Feb 2026). One in five pet owners has gone into debt for vet care (MarketWatch, 2026). Pet insurance claims rose 22% in a single year (NAPHIA, 2026).

We believe informed owners make better decisions — for their pets and their wallets.

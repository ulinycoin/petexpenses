How Much Does a Bernedoodle Cost Per Year?

Last updated May 2026 Data: APPA, AVMA, NAPHIA Dog guide
Quick Answer

The average annual cost of owning a Bernedoodle in the US ranges from $2,856 to $5,592 per year ($238–$466/month). This includes food, routine vet care, pet insurance, grooming, and supplies. Actual costs depend on your Dog’s age, weight, diet, activity level, and location.

Food

Food is typically the largest recurring expense for Bernedoodles. Expect to spend $1080–$1800 per year on quality Dog food. Bernedoodles with food sensitivities may need specialized diets, which can add $0–$100 per year. Larger Bernedoodles eat more and cost more to feed.

Veterinary Care

Routine veterinary care for Bernedoodles costs $480–$1200 per year. This covers annual check-ups, vaccinations, and preventative treatments. Bernedoodles are generally healthy, hardy, and have low risks of major breed-specific genetic diseases. Unexpected emergencies can add thousands in a single visit.

Pet Insurance

Pet insurance for a Bernedoodle costs $576–$1152 per year. Although Bernedoodles are generally healthy, unexpected accidents or illnesses can still happen. Insurance is worth considering since an emergency visit can cost $2,000 to $5,000 — far more than a year's premiums.

Grooming

Bernedoodles have a distinctive wiry coat that needs professional grooming. Budget $420–$840/year for grooming, plus regular at-home brushing.

Supplies

Annual supplies — litter box, scratching post, bed, bowls, toys — run $250–$500. Initial setup in the first year costs more due to one-time purchases.

Why Bernedoodles Cost More Than Average

Bernedoodles cost more than the average dog primarily because of their large size and breed-specific health considerations. Bernedoodles are generally healthy, hardy, and have low risks of major breed-specific genetic diseases. Additionally, bernedoodles have wire-haired coats, which affects annual grooming costs.

How to Save on Bernedoodle Ownership

  • Preventative care is cheaper than emergency care. Regular vet visits catch problems early. Budget for annual check-ups and stay up-to-date on vaccinations.
  • Compare pet insurance plans. Get quotes from at least three providers. Accident-only plans start around $576/year.
  • Buy food and litter in bulk. Subscribe to auto-ship for discounts. Dog food and litter are significantly cheaper per unit in larger quantities.
  • Use preventative dental care. Dental disease is common in dogs and can lead to expensive health issues. At-home dental treats and regular check-ups save money long-term.
  • Choose high-quality food. Better nutrition reduces vet visits from urinary issues, obesity, and allergies.

First-Year vs. Annual Costs

Your first year with a Bernedoodle will be more expensive. Expect to spend an extra $1,689 on:

  • Initial vet visit, vaccinations, and microchipping
  • Spay/neuter surgery
  • Litter box, bed, bowls, scratching post, toys

FAQ About Bernedoodle Costs

How much does a Bernedoodle cost per month?

Monthly costs for a Bernedoodle range from $238 to $466. This includes food, vet care, insurance, grooming, and supplies. Dogs and seniors typically cost more than healthy adults.

Is a Bernedoodle expensive to own compared to other cats?

Bernedoodles are more expensive than average to own. A typical dog costs around $2,800 per year, while bernedoodles at $2,856–$5,592/year cost more due to their large size, breed-specific health needs, and care requirements.

What health issues do Bernedoodles have?

Bernedoodles are generally healthy and hardy, with relatively low risk of genetic conditions. However, like all pets, they still require routine vaccinations, dental care, and preventative vet visits.
